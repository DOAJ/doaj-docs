1. Test Exploring the Subject Tree

Test context
  • role: anonymous
Action Expected Result
Go to the DOAJ journal search page at /search/journals
Select a subject from the subject facet (e.g. Agriculture)
  • The subject is selected in the facet
  • The subject's children are now visible in the subject facet
  • All other top level subjects are still visible in the subject facet
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the subject you selected
  • The search results reflect your subject selection
  • The number of subjects changes in the Subject Search box above.
Select a subject from the second level of subjects (e.g. Animal Culture)
  • The subject is selected in the facet
  • The third level of subjects is now visible in the subject facet
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the lowest level subject you have selected (e.g. Animal Culture), and no other subject filters are present
  • The search results reflect your subject selection
  • The number of subjects in the Subject Search box above has increased/decreased
Select a subject from the third level of subjects (e.g. Cattle)
  • The subject is selected in the facet
  • If present, a 4th level of subjects are now visible in the subject facet
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the lowest level subject you have selected (e.g. Cattle), and no other subject filters are present
  • The search results reflect your subject selection
  • The number of subjects in the Subject Search box above has increased/decreased
Unselect the subject you selected from the third level (e.g. Cattle)
  • The subject is no longer selected in the facet
  • Its parent subjects (2 layers) are still selected
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the new lowest level subject selected (e.g. Animal Culture)
Reselect the subject from the third level of subjects (e.g. Cattle)
Unselect the second level subject, directly above the subject you just selected (e.g. Animal Culture)
  • That subject is no longer selected in the facet, and neither are its children
  • The parent subject (1 layer) is still selected (e.g. Agriculture)
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the new lowest level subject selected (e.g. Agriculture)
  • The number of subjects in the Subject Search box above has increased/decreased
Reselect the second and third level subjects (e.g. Animal Culture, Cattle)
Unselect the top level subject (e.g. Agriculture)
  • No subjects are selected in the facet
  • There is no "selected filter" on the search

2. Multiple Subject Selection

Test context
  • role: anonymous
Action Expected Result
Go to the DOAJ journal search page at /search/journals
Select a subject from the subject facet (e.g. Agriculture)
Select two of the children from the second level (e.g. Forestry and Animal Culture)
  • Both subjects (and their parent) are selected in the facet
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for both second level subjects you selected
Uselect one of the second level subjects (e.g. Animal Culture)
  • The other selected subject is still selected (along with its parent)
  • There is a "selected filter" pill at the top of the search results for the only second level subject (e.g. Forestry)
Reselect one of the second level subjects (e.g. Animal Culture)
Unselect the top level subject (e.g. Agriculture)
  • No subjects are selected in the facet
  • There is no "selected filter" on the search

3. Search for an entry

Test context
  • role: anonymous
Action Expected Result
Go to the DOAJ journal search page at /search/journals
Place your cursor in the Subject search box
Type a single character (e.g. "F")
  • A hint to type 3 characters or more to search is displayed
Enter 3 characters (e.g. "Fin")
  • A list of subjects are displayed. The full tree is shown (with all children expanded), where there is a match somewhere in each branch
  • Your search string is highlighted in bold on each term where it appears
Delete the last character of your search (so you now have, for example, "Fi")
  • A hint to type 3 characters or more to search is displayed
Delete the remaining 2 characters of your search, so there is nothing in the search box
  • The original subject facet content is shown, with a single level of subjects
Enter 3 characters (e.g. "Fin")
Select a subject (try one that is several levels deep, e.g. Technology > Chemical Technology)
  • The subject and any parents are selected in the facet
  • The search box clears
Enter another search of 3 or more characters, which will also return the values you selected in the previous step (e.g. "Bio")
  • A list of subjects are displayed. The full tree is shown (with all children expanded), where there is a match somewhere in each branch
  • Your search string is highlighted in bold on each term where it appears
  • Your previously selected values are displayed as selected within the tree
Select another subject from another part of the tree (e.g. Medicine)
  • All your subjects and any parents are selected in the facet
  • The search box clears
Enter your second search again (e.g. "Bio")
Unselect one of the subjects you had previously selected (e.g. Chemical Technology)
  • Your second subject is still present (e.g. Medicine) and any parents of the subject you unselected are still present (e.g. Technology)
  • The search box clears
Enter a long random string into the search box
  • A message is displayed "No subjects match your search"
